Who I Am

I'm a Computer Science M.S. student at Missouri University of Science and Technology, where my research focuses on AI-driven frameworks for crack width measurement in structural health monitoring imagery. My thesis develops annotation pipelines, segmentation models, and width estimation algorithms to automate structural inspection.

Previously a USGS student contractor co-authoring peer-reviewed work on deep learning for hydrographic feature extraction, and a UF Pathfinder Fellow working on 3D reconstruction and autonomous navigation for greenhouse robotics.

I also built and ship Meridian, a production MCP coordination server for parallel AI coding sessions.

LabVIEW Signal Processing Pipeline
Coursework · ABE6031

Five-stage sensor signal processing pipeline in LabVIEW (ABE6031 Take-Home Final): (1) Z-score outlier removal (σ=2), (2) IIR Butterworth lowpass at 400Hz, (3) FFT with Hanning window + peak magnitude measurement, (4) Fourier series reconstruction at 4 dominant frequencies (~100, 200, 300, 401Hz), (5) RMS error = 4.84 between filtered and reconstructed signals.

DNABERT-2 Fine-tuning for DNA Error Detection
Coursework · CS6001

Graduate ML project (CS6001, w/ Luke Hendrickson) fine-tuning DNABERT-2 for per-base DNA sequencing error detection. 3.2M 150bp reads from chr21 T2T reference with 10% indel + 1% substitution noise.
